Natural Beauty and Resources of Nepal

About Nepal

Nepal is a landlocked country located in South Asia which is bordered by India to the south,east and west and China to the north. The total area of Nepal is 147,516 km2.It is small but beautiful country. Even though it is small in size, it is rich in natural beauty, culture, and biodiversity. Nepal is known around the world for its majestic mountains, peaceful landscapes, and warm hospitality.Nepal is a country blessed with an incredible variety of natural resources. These resources are like a hidden treasure that helps the country grow and provides people with what they need to live.

The country ranges from the lowland Terai plains to the towering Himalayan mountains, creating dramatic landscapes within short distances.Nepal’s economy is strongly connected to agriculture, tourism, and natural resources. The rivers flowing from the Himalayas provide great potential for hydropower, while fertile land supports farming communities across the country. Nepal represents a blend of natural wonder, cultural richness, and resilience.

The Himalayas and Mount Everest

Nepal has the world’s tallest mountains, known as the Himalayas. This includes Mount Everest, the highest peak on Earth. Every year, thousands of people visit to hike and climb these snowy mountains. These areas are famous for beautiful sunrises and the friendly Sherpa people who live there. For the people of Nepal, these mountains are a symbol of their country’s strength.

National parks and Wildlife

Nepal has many protected national parks that preserve wildlife and natural ecosystems.Even though it is a small country, it has set aside more than 20% of its land to keep animals and plants safe.These areas are called National Parks.

Those parks below are home to rare animals such as the one-horned rhinoceros, Bengal tiger, and red panda. The forests, rivers, and grasslands create a diverse environment that supports many species of birds and animals

  1. Chitwan National Park
  2. Sagarmatha National Park
  3. Bardia National Park

Rivers and lake

Nepal is also rich in rivers and lakes that flow from the Himalayas and provide water, electricity, and irrigation.

The below natural water resources support agriculture and tourism. Lakes like Phewa offer beautiful reflections of mountains, making them popular destinations for visitors.

  • Koshi River
  • Gandaki River
  • Phewa Lake

Culture and Festivals

Nepal’s natural beauty is closely connected with its culture and traditions.The people celebrate many special festivals, like Dashain, Tihar, and Buddha Jayanti. These holidays bring families together to celebrate their beliefs and share happiness. Nepal is also the home of Lumbini, which is the peaceful place where Lord Buddha was born. People from all over the world travel there to see where his life began and to find peace.
